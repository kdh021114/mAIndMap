Taewon: The project is interesting because it focuses not only on visualization but also on how conversational structure affects ideation and emotional reflection.
One possible challenge is that graph-based interaction may increase cognitive load for some users, especially during long tasks with many nodes and edges. Although the proposal already discusses this issue, it may help to explain more concretely when graphs become helpful and when they become overwhelming.
The proposal also includes several different goals such as ideation, long-horizon reasoning, and emotional reflection. It may be useful to clarify which task type the contribution primarily focuses on, as the benefits of graph interaction may differ across tasks.
For the user study, it would also be interesting to consider how to measure “better ideation” or “better reflection” reliably. Metrics like idea diversity or context recall are useful, but qualitative analysis of how users actually organize and revisit thoughts may also become important.
